### Important Information
- **When:** 📅 _28th of January 2026, time tbh_
- **Where:** 🌐 _Online (via Zoom)_ 🧑‍💻
- **Price:** 💰 _Free_

The next inaugural HAAM-Radio Webinar will be taking place online on the **28th of January, 2025**! This webinar will focus on advances in reconstructing past demography, interactions, and social organisation in Africa. 

We are delighted to announce the two invited speakers:
- **“Insights into population genomics and human history in Africa and the African Diaspora"**
Cesar Fortes-Lima @Human Evolution Program, Department of Organismal Biology, Evolutionary Biology Centre, Uppsala University, Sweden

- **“Genomic Insights into Initial and Secondary Bantu Migrations in Western Central Africa”**
Oguzhan Parasayan @Institute Pasteur, Paris, France

Additionally, the following early career researchers will be presenting their work:
- **“Population Mobilities and Genetic Diversity of Eastern Africans”**
Emmanuel Kipruto Ngetich @University of Minnesota Twin Cities, Minneapolis, MN, United States

- **“The Green Sahara genomic landscape: insights from whole genome analysis of modern northern African and Sahelian Populations”**
Flavia Risi @Population genetics group, Sapienza University of Rome, Italy

- **“Linguistic perspectives on the population history of Southern Africa”**
Hilde Gunnink @BantUGent (UGent Centre for Bantu Studies), Ghent University, Belgium

- **“The palaeoecological shift that enabled our species to leave Africa”**
Michela Leonardi @Natural History Museum, London, UK
